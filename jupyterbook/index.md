# Social Security Taxation Reform Options: A PolicyEngine Analysis

This Jupyter Book presents a comprehensive analysis of six different approaches to reforming Social Security benefits taxation using PolicyEngine's microsimulation capabilities. The analysis provides 10-year budgetary impact estimates for each policy option, enabling policymakers to make informed decisions about Social Security taxation reform.

## Overview

Social Security benefits taxation was first introduced in 1983 and has evolved into a complex two-tier system that creates administrative challenges for taxpayers and the IRS. This analysis examines six different reform approaches, each offering different trade-offs between revenue generation, administrative simplicity, and distributional effects.

## Policy Options Analyzed

1. **Complete Repeal**: Eliminating taxation of Social Security benefits entirely
2. **Flat 85% Taxation**: Taxing 85% of all Social Security benefits regardless of income
3. **Flat Taxation with Senior Deduction Extension**: Combining flat taxation with permanent extension of the bonus senior deduction
4. **Tax Credit Approach**: Replacing the bonus senior deduction with a nonrefundable tax credit
5. **Roth-Style Swap**: Taxing employer payroll contributions instead of Social Security benefits
6. **Phased Roth-Style Swap**: Gradually transitioning from benefit taxation to payroll contribution taxation

## Methodology

Our analysis employs PolicyEngine's microsimulation framework using the Enhanced Current Population Survey (CPS) dataset. This approach provides:
- Precise revenue impact estimates for each policy option
- Year-by-year analysis over a 10-year budget window (2026-2035)
- Household-level analysis of distributional effects
- Transparent, replicable methodology

## Key Findings

The analysis reveals significant variation in revenue impacts across policy options, from complete elimination of Social Security taxation revenue to timing shifts that maintain revenue generation through different mechanisms. Each approach presents distinct trade-offs between administrative simplification, revenue generation, and distributional effects.
