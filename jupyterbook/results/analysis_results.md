# Analysis Results

This chapter presents the quantitative results of our PolicyEngine analysis of the six Social Security taxation reform options. The analysis provides 10-year budgetary impact estimates for each policy option, allowing for comparison of revenue effects and policy trade-offs.

## Data Overview

Our analysis generated 100 total observations across the six policy options:
- **Policy 1**: Complete repeal of Social Security benefits taxation (10 years)
- **Policy 2**: Flat 85% taxation of Social Security benefits (10 years)  
- **Policy 3**: Flat 85% taxation with permanent senior deduction extension (10 years)
- **Policy 4**: Tax credit approach with 5 variants ($300, $600, $900, $1,200, $1,500) × 10 years = 50 observations
- **Policy 5**: Roth-style swap (10 years)
- **Policy 6**: Phased Roth-style swap (10 years)

## Summary Statistics

The analysis covers the period 2026-2035, providing year-by-year estimates for each policy option. Results are presented in billions of dollars, with positive values indicating revenue increases and negative values indicating revenue decreases compared to current law.

## Policy-by-Policy Results

### Policy 1: Complete Repeal
This option eliminates all revenue from Social Security benefits taxation, resulting in significant negative revenue impacts throughout the analysis period.

### Policy 2: Flat 85% Taxation  
This option simplifies the current two-tier system while maintaining revenue generation through flat taxation of Social Security benefits.

### Policy 3: Flat Taxation with Senior Deduction Extension
This option combines flat taxation with permanent extension of the bonus senior deduction, creating interaction effects between the two provisions.

### Policy 4: Tax Credit Approach
This option includes five variants with different credit amounts, allowing analysis of how credit design affects overall revenue impacts.

### Policy 5: Roth-Style Swap
This option shifts taxation timing from retirement to working years by taxing employer payroll contributions instead of Social Security benefits.

### Policy 6: Phased Roth-Style Swap
This option implements the Roth-style swap gradually over time, providing a smoother transition period.

## Key Findings

### Revenue Impact Patterns
- **Policy 1** shows the largest negative revenue impacts due to complete elimination of Social Security taxation
- **Policies 2-4** show varying revenue effects depending on their specific design features
- **Policies 5-6** show timing shifts rather than net revenue changes

### Year-by-Year Trends
Revenue impacts generally increase over time as Social Security benefits grow and more beneficiaries become subject to taxation under current law.

### Policy Comparison
The analysis reveals significant differences in revenue impacts across policy options, highlighting the importance of careful policy design in achieving desired revenue outcomes.

## Sensitivity Analysis

### Credit Amount Sensitivity (Policy 4)
The analysis includes five different credit amounts for Policy 4, allowing assessment of how credit design affects overall revenue impacts.

### Time Period Analysis
The 10-year analysis window provides insights into both short-term implementation effects and medium-term sustainability of each policy option.

## Limitations and Caveats

### Static Analysis
The analysis assumes no behavioral responses to policy changes, which may underestimate or overestimate actual revenue impacts.

### Economic Assumptions
Results are based on current economic projections and may differ under different economic scenarios.

### Implementation Considerations
The analysis focuses on revenue impacts and does not address administrative implementation costs or taxpayer compliance issues.
